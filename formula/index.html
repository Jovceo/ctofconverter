Temperature Conversion Formulas

Temperature can be measured using different scales, each with its own applications and regions of use. The most common temperature scales are Celsius, Fahrenheit, and Kelvin.

Celsius to Fahrenheit

°F = (°C × 9/5) + 32

This formula converts temperature from Celsius (°C) to Fahrenheit (°F). Multiply the Celsius temperature by 9/5 (or 1.8) and then add 32.

Example: Convert 20°C to Fahrenheit: (20 × 9/5) + 32 = 36 + 32 = 68°F

Fahrenheit to Celsius

°C = (°F - 32) × 5/9

This formula converts temperature from Fahrenheit (°F) to Celsius (°C). Subtract 32 from the Fahrenheit temperature, then multiply by 5/9.

Example: Convert 68°F to Celsius: (68 - 32) × 5/9 = 36 × 5/9 = 20°C

Celsius to Kelvin

K = °C + 273.15

This formula converts temperature from Celsius (°C) to Kelvin (K). Add 273.15 to the Celsius temperature.

Example: Convert 25°C to Kelvin: 25 + 273.15 = 298.15K

°C ≈ (°F - 30) ÷ 2

Example: 20°C ≈ (20×2)+30 = 70°F (actual 68°F)

Derivation of the Formulas

The temperature conversion formulas are derived from the relationship between the different temperature scales. The key reference points are the freezing and boiling points of water:

  • Water freezes at 0°C, 32°F, and 273.15K
  • Water boils at 100°C, 212°F, and 373.15K
1

Determine the Ratio Between Celsius and Fahrenheit

The difference between the freezing and boiling points is 100° in Celsius and 180° in Fahrenheit.

Ratio = 180°F / 100°C = 9/5 = 1.8
2

Account for the Offset

The Fahrenheit scale has a 32° offset at the freezing point (0°C = 32°F).

So we add 32 to the converted value: °F = (°C × 9/5) + 32
3

Reverse Formula (Fahrenheit to Celsius)

To derive the reverse formula, we solve for °C:

°F = (°C × 9/5) + 32
°F - 32 = °C × 9/5
(°F - 32) × 5/9 = °C
4

Celsius to Kelvin Relationship

The Kelvin scale uses the same increment as Celsius but starts at absolute zero (-273.15°C).

K = °C + 273.15

Easy Conversion Methods

For everyday use, you can use these simple approximation methods that are easy to remember and calculate mentally:

Quick Celsius to Fahrenheit Approximation

°F ≈ (°C × 2) + 30

This method is reasonably accurate for temperatures between 10°C and 30°C. For example:

  • 20°C ≈ (20×2)+30 = 70°F (actual 68°F)
  • 15°C ≈ (15×2)+30 = 60°F (actual 59°F)

Quick Fahrenheit to Celsius Approximation

°C ≈ (°F - 30) ÷ 2

This is the reverse of the previous method. For example:

  • 70°F ≈ (70-30)÷2 = 20°C (actual 21.1°C)
  • 60°F ≈ (60-30)÷2 = 15°C (actual 15.6°C)

While these approximations are not exact, they're useful for quick estimations in daily life when precision isn't critical.

How to Convert 30°C to Fahrenheit

Let's walk through the conversion of 30°C to Fahrenheit step by step:

1

Start with the formula

°F = (°C × 9/5) + 32
2

Plug in the Celsius value

°F = (30 × 9/5) + 32
3

Multiply 30 by 9/5

30 × 9/5 = 270/5 = 54
4

Add 32 to the result

54 + 32 = 86
5

Final result

30°C = 86°F

Using the approximation method: (30 × 2) + 30 = 90°F, which is close to the exact value of 86°F.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do you convert Celsius to Fahrenheit easily?

The easiest way to convert Celsius to Fahrenheit mentally is to use the approximation formula: °F ≈ (°C × 2) + 30. This method is not exact but gives a close estimate for everyday temperatures. For example, 20°C ≈ (20×2)+30 = 70°F (actual value is 68°F).

What is the formula for Fahrenheit to Celsius?

The exact formula to convert Fahrenheit to Celsius is: °C = (°F - 32) × 5/9. First subtract 32 from the Fahrenheit temperature, then multiply the result by 5/9. For example, to convert 68°F to Celsius: (68-32) × 5/9 = 36 × 5/9 = 20°C.

What is the equation from Celsius to Fahrenheit?

The equation to convert Celsius to Fahrenheit is: °F = (°C × 9/5) + 32. Multiply the Celsius temperature by 9/5 (or 1.8), then add 32 to the result. For example, 25°C = (25 × 9/5) + 32 = 45 + 32 = 77°F.

What is the quick way to convert Fahrenheit to Celsius?

The quick mental approximation for Fahrenheit to Celsius is: °C ≈ (°F - 30) ÷ 2. For example, 70°F ≈ (70-30)÷2 = 20°C (actual value is 21.1°C). This method is less accurate than the exact formula but useful for quick estimations.

Why is -40°C equal to -40°F?

-40°C is equal to -40°F because this is the point where the two temperature scales intersect. If we set °C = °F in the conversion formula: F = (F × 9/5) + 32 → F - (9/5)F = 32 → (-4/5)F = 32 → F = -40. So -40°C = -40°F.

How do I convert Celsius to Kelvin?

To convert Celsius to Kelvin, use the formula: K = °C + 273.15. Simply add 273.15 to the Celsius temperature. For example, 25°C = 25 + 273.15 = 298.15K. The Kelvin scale uses the same increment as Celsius but starts at absolute zero (-273.15°C).
