What is gas mark 4 in a fan oven?

Gas mark 4 is equivalent to 180°C in a fan oven. This temperature is perfect for baking cakes, cookies, and roasting vegetables.

450 fahrenheit to celsius fan oven conversion

450°F converts to approximately 230°C in a conventional oven. For a fan oven, reduce by 20°C to get 210°C.

How to convert 180°C conventional to fan oven

To convert 180°C from conventional to fan oven, reduce the temperature by 20°C. The fan oven equivalent is 160°C.

Food-Specific Conversion Guide

Breads & Pizza

  • Artisan bread: 230°C fan (250°C conventional)
  • Pizza: 220-240°C fan, bake directly on stone
  • Quick breads: Reduce time by 15%

Tip: Steam injection helps crust development in fan ovens

Cakes & Pastries

  • Sponge cakes: 160°C fan, check 5 min early
  • Butter cakes: 170°C fan, use middle rack
  • Puff pastry: 200°C fan for maximum rise

Tip: Reduce sugar slightly as fan ovens promote browning

Meats & Roasts

  • Whole chicken: 180°C fan, 20% less time
  • Beef roast: 160°C fan for medium-rare
  • Fish: 150°C fan, watch carefully

Tip: Always use meat thermometer for perfect results

Professional Baking Tips for Fan Ovens

1. Oven Thermometer is Essential

Most ovens run hot or cold. For precision baking, place an independent oven thermometer in the center of your oven to verify actual temperature.

2. Adjust Baking Times

As a general rule, reduce baking times by 10-15% when using fan mode. Start checking for doneness earlier than recipe suggests.

3. Rack Position Matters

Middle rack works best for most baking. For multiple racks, rotate pans halfway through baking for even results.

4. Avoid Overcrowding

Fan ovens need good air circulation. Leave at least 2 inches between pans and avoid covering oven racks with foil.

5. Humidity Control

Fan ovens can dry foods faster. For moist cakes, place a pan of water in the bottom of the oven during baking.

6. High Altitude Adjustments

At elevations above 900m (3000ft), you may need additional temperature reductions of 5-10°C in fan ovens.

Fan Oven Conversion FAQs

Why do fan ovens need lower temperatures?

Fan ovens circulate hot air continuously, creating more efficient heat transfer to your food. The 20°C reduction compensates for this increased efficiency, preventing overcooking or burning.

How do I know if my fan oven runs hot or cold?

Place an oven thermometer in the center of your oven. Set to 180°C fan and let preheat for 20 minutes. Check thermometer reading through the window. If it's more than 10°C different, you may need to adjust your settings.

Should I reduce both temperature and time?

Yes, for most recipes you should reduce temperature by 20°C (from conventional to fan) and reduce time by 10-15% (start checking early).

Are all fan ovens the same?

While the 20°C rule works for most fan ovens, there can be variations. Standard fan ovens typically need -20°C adjustment, while multi-function/combi ovens may need -10 to -30°C adjustments.

What is 425°F in a fan oven?

425°F converts to approximately 220°C in a conventional oven. For a fan oven, reduce by 20°C to get 200°C.

How to convert gas mark to fan oven?
