220°C to Fahrenheit Conversion

220°C = 428°F

Perfect temperature for pizza, roasting, and air frying

Temperature Conversion Formula

Precise conversion formula:

°F = (°C × 9/5) + 32

220°C conversion step-by-step:

1. Multiply by 9/5: 220 × 9/5 = 396

2. Add 32: 396 + 32 = 428

3. Final result: 220°C = 428°F

Practical Uses of 220°C/428°F

Traditional Ovens

Ideal for:

  • Neapolitan pizza: 220°C for 8-10 minutes (with pizza stone)
  • Roast potatoes: 45-60 minutes for perfect crispness
  • Sheet pan dinners: Even cooking for meats and vegetables

Pro tip: Most home ovens run 10-20°C cooler than set temperature. Use an oven thermometer!

Air Fryers

Recommended adjustments:

  • Temperature: Reduce by 15-20°C (200°C in air fryer)
  • Cooking time: Reduce by 20-30% vs conventional oven
  • Best for: Chicken wings (18-22 mins), Fries (12-15 mins)

Note: Air fryer models vary - check your manual for specific recommendations.

Fan/Convection Ovens

Key differences:

  • Reduce temperature: 200°C for fan oven (≈220°C conventional)
  • Cooking time: May be 10-15% faster
  • Best results: Multiple rack cooking, baked goods

Exception: For steam-assisted ovens, you may not need to reduce temperature.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q1: Why does my food burn at 220°C in my new oven?

A: Modern ovens are more accurate. Try reducing temperature by 10°C or checking if you have a fan-assisted oven (which requires lower temps).

Q2: How long to preheat to 220°C?

A: Typically 15-20 minutes, but use your oven's indicator light. For precision, wait 5 extra minutes after the light goes off.

Q3: Can I use 220°C for frozen foods?

A: Yes, but add 10-15% cooking time. For air fryers, follow package instructions but reduce temp by 15-20°C.

Q4: Is 220°C suitable for all pizza types?

A: Best for thin crust. For deep dish, reduce to 200°C and cook longer. For authentic Neapolitan, you'd need 300°C+.

Q5: Why do British recipes use different temperatures?

A: UK ovens are typically fan-assisted. If a British recipe says 220°C, it means 220°C fan (≈240°C conventional).
