How to Convert 120 Celsius to Fahrenheit: Exact Formula

To convert from Celsius to Fahrenheit, use the following formula:

°F = (°C × 9/5) + 32

Step-by-Step Calculation

  1. Start with the temperature in Celsius: 120°C
  2. Multiply by 9/5: 120 × 9/5 = 120 × 1.8 = 216
  3. Add 32: 216 + 32 = 248
  4. Result: 120°C = 248°F

About 120°C / 248°F: Temperature Facts and Applications

120°C (248°F) is a specialized temperature that serves important roles in both culinary and industrial settings. Unlike higher baking temperatures, 120°C is considered a low to moderate heat that's perfect for processes requiring gentle, sustained heating.

Important Safety Note: 120°C is NOT a body temperature or fever reading. Normal human body temperature ranges from 36.5°C to 37.5°C (97.7°F to 99.5°F). Temperatures of 120°C can cause severe burns instantly.

In scientific terms, 120°C sits well above water's boiling point (100°C/212°F) but below most standard baking temperatures, making it ideal for specific applications where controlled, lower heat is necessary.

Practical Applications of 120°C (248°F)

Food Dehydration & Preservation

120°C is ideal for food dehydration processes:

  • Fruit dehydration: Perfect for making dried apples, bananas, and mangoes
  • Herb drying: Preserves flavor while removing moisture efficiently
  • Jerky making: Slowly dehydrates meat while maintaining food safety
  • Vegetable chips: Creates crispy snacks without burning
Fan Forced Oven Conversion

For fan forced (convection) ovens, reduce the temperature by 20°C:

  • Conventional oven: 120°C = 248°F
  • Fan forced oven: 100°C ≈ 212°F

This adjustment ensures even cooking in fan-assisted ovens where air circulation increases cooking efficiency. Check our fan oven conversion chart for more details.

Industrial & Scientific Uses

120°C serves important functions in various industries:

  • Plastic curing: Some thermoset plastics cure at this temperature
  • Laboratory sterilization: Used for heat-resistant equipment
  • Chemical processes: Specific reactions occur optimally at 120°C
  • Material testing: Standard temperature for certain quality tests
Low-Temperature Cooking

While lower than typical roasting temperatures, 120°C has culinary uses:

  • Slow-cooked dishes: Gentle heat for tough cuts of meat
  • Food warming: Keeps prepared dishes hot without continuing to cook
  • Proofing bread: Some bread recipes use this temperature for final proofing
  • Candy making: Specific stages in sugar work

Frequently Asked Questions

What is 120 degrees Celsius equal to in Fahrenheit?

120 degrees Celsius is equal to 248 degrees Fahrenheit. This conversion is calculated using the standard formula: °F = (°C × 9/5) + 32.

What temperature is 120 degrees C in oven settings?

120°C in an oven equals 248°F. This is considered a low oven temperature, typically used for dehydrating foods, slow cooking, or keeping dishes warm without further cooking them.

Is 120°C a fever temperature?

No, absolutely not. 120°C (248°F) is not a body temperature. Normal human body temperature ranges from 36.5°C to 37.5°C (97.7°F to 99.5°F). A temperature of 120°C would be instantly fatal. This is likely a common search mistake - people probably meant to search for 38°C or similar fever temperatures.

How do I convert negative 120 Celsius to Fahrenheit?

To convert -120°C to Fahrenheit, use the same formula: °F = (-120 × 9/5) + 32 = -184°F. This extremely cold temperature is used in specialized scientific and industrial applications.

What's the difference between 120°C in conventional vs fan forced ovens?

In a conventional oven, 120°C = 248°F. In a fan forced (convection) oven, you should reduce the temperature by approximately 20°C, so you'd use 100°C (212°F) to achieve similar results due to the improved air circulation.

Can I use 120°C for baking cakes or bread?

120°C is generally too low for most baking. Typical baking temperatures range from 150°C to 200°C (302°F to 392°F). However, 120°C can be used for dehydrating fruit, making jerky, or slow-cooking certain dishes.
